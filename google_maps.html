Google Maps Usability Study



Background

Though Google Maps has steadily undergone improvements since it's launch, there are still some usability vulnerabilities throughout the application. This usability test sought to both uncover these issues as well as make suggestions on how to enhance the experience based on our research.

The Process

Before diving into the Google Maps interface to determine flaws in the design, we began by familiarizing ourselves with the entire application, highlighting particularly the lesser known features. This enabled us to target specific features when it came time to conduct a heuristic analysis and user tests. Based on our findings during the interface exploration and our heuritstic analysis, we crafted 3 testing scenarios that we would have our test participants attempt to complete.

My Role

For this project, I was responsible for contributing to the creation of the usability testing scenarios. Once the scenarios were developed, I wrote the instructions for each scenario into our test booklet. I was also responsible for conducting both face-to-face usability tests and remote usability tests. Upon the completion of all user testing, I compiled all user insights and authored the recommendations section of our final report.
